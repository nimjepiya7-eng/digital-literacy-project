# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today. It involves tricking users into authorizing fraudulent transactions or revealing sensitive banking details.

A typical scenario begins when a victim receives a call, message, or link from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. The fraudster may claim that the user needs to “verify” their account, receive a refund, or accept a payment. 

Step-by-step, the fraud usually happens as follows:
1. The scammer contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or social media.
2. They create urgency or trust (e.g., fake offer, problem with account).
3. The victim is asked to click a link or approve a “collect request” on a UPI app.
4. The victim unknowingly enters their UPI PIN.
5. Money is instantly transferred from the victim’s account to the fraudster.

College students are often targeted due to frequent use of digital payments and limited awareness about such scams.

The consequences can include immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering funds. In some cases, personal data may also be misused for further fraud.

This highlights the importance of digital awareness and safe online practices while using payment platforms.
