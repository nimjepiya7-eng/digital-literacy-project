# digital-literacy-project

INTRODUCTION

The Digital Literacy Portfolio project is designed to help students develop essential skills required to navigate today’s digital world safely, responsibly, and professionally. In this scenario, I have been selected as a Student Digital Ambassador, a role that involves guiding peers in building a strong online presence, practicing safe digital communication, and understanding common online threats.
Through this project, I completed five structured tasks, each aligned with a module of digital literacy. These tasks included creating a professional GitHub profile, writing formal emails following proper etiquette, designing awareness resources such as checklists, and analyzing real-world cybercrime scenarios. All the work has been systematically organized into a single GitHub repository, making it easy to access, review, and share.
The objective of this project is not only to build technical and communication skills but also to promote responsible digital behavior among students. By working on practical tasks, I gained a better understanding of online safety, digital identity management, and professional communication.
Overall, this project highlights the importance of being digitally aware and responsible, enabling students to use technology effectively while avoiding risks in an increasingly connected world.

 
TASK 1 
Create a Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
For this task, I used Canva to design a one-page infographic on digital literacy. Canva was chosen because of its user-friendly interface, ready-made templates, and visually appealing design elements, which made it easier to create a professional-looking output.
The infographic highlights three key aspects of digital literacy: understanding what digital literacy means, practicing safe internet usage, and maintaining a professional online presence. It explains digital literacy as the ability to use digital tools effectively, responsibly, and safely. The design also includes tips such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and being mindful of what we share online. Additionally, it emphasizes the importance of having a clean and professional digital footprint, especially for future academic and career opportunities.
While creating the infographic, one challenge I faced was selecting the right amount of content to fit into a single page without making it look cluttered. It was important to keep the design simple yet informative. An interesting part of this task was experimenting with icons, colors, and layouts to make the information visually engaging and easy to understand.
Overall, this activity helped me understand how to present important information in a concise and creative way.

TASK 2
Build Your Student Digital Portfolio
For this task, I created and updated my profiles on three key platforms: GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. These platforms are widely used by students, professionals, and recruiters to evaluate skills, projects, and academic background.
GitHub is mainly used to showcase coding projects and technical work. I created a profile README that includes my name, branch, year, and learning goals. This will serve as a portfolio of my projects and help demonstrate my practical skills. LinkedIn is a professional networking platform where I added my education details, including my current degree, institution, and expected graduation year. It helps in building connections with professionals and discovering internship opportunities. Kaggle is a platform for data science and machine learning, where users can practice datasets, participate in competitions, and improve analytical skills.
Over the next four years, I plan to regularly update my GitHub with projects and assignments, actively engage on LinkedIn by networking and sharing achievements, and use Kaggle to build strong data analysis and problem-solving skills. This will help me create a strong digital presence and improve my career opportunities in the future.

 
TASK 3 
Explore Coding & Collaboration Platforms
In this task, I explored two important categories of digital tools: coding practice platforms and cloud collaboration tools. I used platforms like HackerRank and LeetCode for coding practice, and Google Docs and Google Drive for collaboration.
HackerRank and LeetCode are widely used platforms that help students improve their programming and problem-solving skills. On these platforms, I practiced basic problems related to arrays, loops, and conditional statements. This helped me strengthen my logical thinking and understand how to approach coding questions step by step, which is essential for interviews and competitive programming.
For collaboration, I used Google Docs and Google Drive. These tools allow multiple users to work on the same document in real time. I created and edited documents, shared files, and organized them in folders. This made it easier to manage my project work and collaborate efficiently without needing to be physically present with others.
Overall, this task helped me understand the importance of using the right digital tools for learning and teamwork. In the future, I plan to regularly practice coding on these platforms and use cloud tools for group projects and assignments to improve productivity and collaboration skills.
 
TASK 4
Draft a Professional Email & Etiquette Guide
In this task, I focused on understanding and applying proper email etiquette through real communication scenarios. I wrote professional emails such as requesting an assignment extension from a professor and expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity. These scenarios helped me practice formal communication in academic and professional contexts.
One of the key learnings from this task was the importance of structure in an email. A clear subject line, polite greeting, well-organized body, and proper sign-off are essential to make the message effective and respectful. I also learned to keep the tone formal, avoid slang, and be concise while clearly stating the purpose of the email. Using respectful language such as “I hope you are doing well” and “Thank you for your consideration” helps create a positive impression.
Another important learning was the need to provide all necessary details without making the email too lengthy. Clarity and professionalism are crucial, especially when communicating with professors or employers.
Overall, this task improved my confidence in writing formal emails and taught me how to communicate responsibly and professionally online, which is an important skill for academic and career success.

 
TASK 5
Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide
While working on the cybercrime awareness task, one thing that surprised me the most was how simple and convincing many online scams can be. In the case of UPI fraud, I realized that victims are often tricked not through complex hacking, but through basic psychological tactics like creating urgency, fear, or trust. Even a small action, such as approving a payment request without checking details, can lead to immediate financial loss. This made me understand that awareness is just as important as technical security.
I also found it surprising that many people are unaware that receiving money does not require entering a UPI PIN. This misunderstanding is commonly exploited by scammers, especially targeting students and first-time digital users.
One habit I will definitely change is being more cautious before clicking on links or responding to unknown calls and messages. Earlier, I did not always verify the source of links or requests, but now I understand the importance of double-checking everything, especially when it involves personal or financial information.
Overall, this task has made me more alert and responsible while using digital platforms, and I will also help spread awareness among my peers to prevent such cybercrimes.
 
CONCLUSION

Overall, the Digital Literacy Portfolio project has been a valuable learning experience that enhanced both my technical and communication skills. Through the five tasks, I gained practical knowledge about creating a professional digital presence using platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn, and learned how to effectively use digital tools for coding practice and collaboration. I also developed a better understanding of formal communication by applying proper email etiquette in real-life scenarios.
Additionally, researching cybercrime and creating awareness resources helped me become more cautious and responsible while using online platforms. I now better understand common digital threats and how to avoid them.
This project also improved my ability to organize and present information clearly through visual designs and structured content. Overall, it has made me more confident, digitally aware, and prepared to guide others as a Student Digital Ambassador in promoting safe and responsible technology use.
